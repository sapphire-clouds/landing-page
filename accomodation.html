Accommodation Guide for Estonia

Whether you're seeking a luxurious stay in the heart of Tallinn's old town or a cozy countryside retreat, Estonia has something to suit every traveler's taste and budget. Here's a guide to help you navigate the various accommodation options in this enchanting Baltic country.

1. Hotels:

a. Luxury Hotels:

  • Hotel Telegraaf (Tallinn): A 5-star hotel located in the heart of Tallinn's historic center, offering luxurious rooms, spa facilities, and gourmet dining.
  • Swissôtel Tallinn: Situated in Tallinn's business district, this 5-star hotel provides panoramic views of the city and modern amenities.

b. Boutique Hotels:

  • Hotel St. Petersbourg (Tallinn): Estonia's oldest hotel, offering a blend of historic charm and contemporary comfort.
  • Vihula Manor Country Club & Spa (Northern Estonia): Set amidst beautiful countryside, this boutique hotel offers a unique experience with its historic manor house and spa facilities.

c. Budget-Friendly Hotels:

  • Hotel Bern (Tallinn): Located near Tallinn's old town, this hotel provides comfortable accommodation at affordable prices.
  • Hotel Sophia (Tartu): A budget-friendly option in Estonia's second-largest city, known for its cozy atmosphere and convenient location.

2. Guesthouses and B&Bs:

For a more intimate and authentic experience, consider staying at a guesthouse or B&B.

  • Pärnu: Villa Artis: A charming guesthouse located near Pärnu's beach, offering cozy rooms and personalized service.
  • Saaremaa: Padaste Manor: A luxurious countryside retreat on the island of Saaremaa, offering elegant rooms, gourmet dining, and serene surroundings.

3. Hostels:

For budget-conscious travelers and backpackers, hostels are a great option.

  • Tallinn: Tallinn Backpackers: Located in the heart of the old town, offering affordable dormitory and private rooms.
  • Tartu: Hektor Design Hostel: A stylish and modern hostel in Tartu, offering both dormitory and private rooms.

4. Unique Accommodation Options:

For a truly memorable experience, consider staying at one of Estonia's unique accommodation options.

  • Estonian Islands: Koguva Village: Experience traditional Estonian village life by staying in a historic farmhouse on the island of Muhu.
  • Lahemaa National Park: Altja Fisherman's Village: Stay in a traditional coastal cottage and explore the natural beauty of Lahemaa National Park.

Tips for Booking Accommodation in Estonia:

  • Book in Advance: Especially during the peak tourist season (summer months), it's advisable to book your accommodation well in advance.
  • Check Reviews: Before booking, read reviews from other travelers to ensure the accommodation meets your expectations.
  • Location: Consider the location of your accommodation in relation to the attractions you plan to visit. Estonia has a well-connected public transportation system, but staying centrally can save time and money.
